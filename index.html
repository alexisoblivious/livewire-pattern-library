Model

Modal is a user interface component that displays content, information, or options in a pop-up or overlay, typically overlaid on top of the main content of a webpage. Modals are used to focus the user's attention on a specific task or piece of information while dimming or graying out the background content to provide visual hierarchy and reduce distractions.

Line Graphs

Line graphs are used for the comparison of continous data types. They are typically used to track the change in value of multiple objects over a time period.

Bar Graphs

Bar graphs are used for the comparison of discontinuous data across one or more groups. You can have regular bar charts which compared a single value across different groups, or grouped bar charts which compare multiple data values across different groups.

Single Value Charts

Single value charts are unique in that they display only a single value. They can be represented in multiple ways, including progress bars, single results, or results with associated changes. They are great for displaying important values, significant findings, or results of computation.

Area Charts

Area charts are used for displaying the composition of a whole, with continuous data. These types of charts are great for showing how different groups make up the whole composition and how they change over time.

Scatter Plots

Scatter plots are used for showing the relationship between two variables. Each axis will represent one of these variables and multiple points will be plotted against each axis. Typically scatter plots aim to find a trend: linear, non-linear, positive, or negative. Statistical analysis can be performed to see how well they fit this pattern.

Pie Charts

Pie charts are used when you would like to graph the composition of groups within a whole with static data. The entire chart represents 100% and each group within the pie takes up a percentage of that whole.

Donut Charts

Donut charts are similar to pie charts in that they graph the composition of groups within a whole with static data. They are different in their visual appearance and the types of values they can hold. Donut charts always have an empty circle in the middle. As well, they are able to chart both positive and negative values, however it this circumstance they would not be representing composition of a whole.
